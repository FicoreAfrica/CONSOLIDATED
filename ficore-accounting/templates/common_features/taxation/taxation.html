Updated as of July 02, 2025

{{ t('tax_key_changes', default='Key Changes for Small Businesses and Individuals') }}

  • Tax Exemption for Low Earners: If you earn less than ₦800,000 annually, you’re exempt from personal income tax.
  • Small Business Relief: If your business earns under ₦25 million yearly, you pay 0% company income tax.
  • VAT Updates: The VAT rate stays at 7.5%, but essentials like food, medicine, and electricity are now zero-rated, meaning no VAT on these purchases. You can recover input VAT on related business costs.
  • E-Invoicing Mandate: All businesses must use e-invoicing systems provided by the Nigeria Revenue Service (NRS) for VAT compliance.
  • Development Levy: A 2%-4% levy applies to fund national projects, based on your income or turnover.
  • New Tax Ombudsman: An independent office is available to help resolve tax disputes.

{{ t('tax_actions_to_take', default='Actions to Take') }}

  1. Get Informed: Learn about these changes and how they affect you or your small business.
  2. Assess Impact: Check how your finances and operations are affected, especially with e-invoicing and VAT recovery.
  3. Update Strategy: Adjust your tax planning to benefit from exemptions and prepare for e-invoicing.
  4. Implement Changes: Update your records and software to comply with new rules by July 2025.
  5. Seek Support: Contact the Tax Ombudsman or NRS for help with compliance issues.
  6. Stay Updated: Regularly check for new guidelines from the NRS.
